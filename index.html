Hello, I’m Christopher Okoh, a Master's in Robotics Engineering student advised by Emanuele Secco Lindo, Associate Professor at the Robotics Lab at the School of Computer Science and Environment, Liverpool Hope University. My focus is on real-time autonomy, sensor fusion, embedded AI, and Virtual Reality, especially for uncertain, harsh, and edge-case environments.

I've worked across industry and academia, with engineering and research experience at Barcelona Tech, Black in Robotics, CiTech Automation, and LHU. Whether I’m writing firmware for robots, training deep learning models, or developing a new PCB, I’m always thinking of ways to translate ideas into working systems.

Right now, I’m building an integrated deep learning framework for the Summit-XL robotic platform, applying modern computer vision techniques for low-cost human-robot interaction, and experimenting with how neural networks can control robots in safety-constrained scenarios. If that sounds like your kind of thing, I’d love to chat.
